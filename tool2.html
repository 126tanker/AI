How a Byte Stores Data

Capacitance → Bits → Binary

Each "battery" below represents a tiny area of capacitance in memory. When it is charged, it represents a 1. When it is not charged, it represents a 0.

The numbers shown are the powers of 2 that each bit contributes to the byte’s total value.

00000000
Decimal value: 0

The leftmost battery is the most significant bit (128), and the rightmost is the least significant bit (1). The total value of the byte is found by adding the values of all charged batteries.
