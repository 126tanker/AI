How a Byte Stores Data

Capacitance → Bits → Binary

Inside computer memory, data is stored using tiny areas of capacitance. Each area can be either:

  • Charged → represents a 1
  • Not charged → represents a 0

Eight of these charged or uncharged areas together form a byte. Click the memory cells below to charge or discharge them and see how the pattern of 1s and 0s changes.

00000000
Decimal value: 0

The leftmost cell is the most significant bit (MSB), and the rightmost is the least significant bit (LSB). When the pattern of charged and uncharged capacitance changes, the number the byte represents also changes.
